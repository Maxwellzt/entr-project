How We Handle Debris



Material Analysis

There are many different types of debris on our orbit today, ranging from prior launch waste, to broken down satelites, meteorites, asteriods and much more We are able to scan every peice of debris before it enters the mouth of the ZZB-963x and determine exactly what it is comprised of within milliseconds of contact. Having this information, we are then able to determine presice cut points on the item and using our particle accelerated lazers, slice away the recycleable materials found in the substance. if the material does not pass a threshold of quality for recycling, we engage our patented filtration rutter, which exposes the waste collection portion of the space craft.



Material Collection

Space dusters rely's on the collaboration with many industry leaders in technology and material sciences, and NASA has paired with our team in order to find a solution to effectively capture the debris after it has been cut down in the analysis phase. The collection modules are filled with NASA's Aerogel, whos chemical and physical properties allow for the capturing of microscopic particles, all the way to larger metals and rocks. once the collection modules have reached a specific mass, the recyclable module detatches on a path to earth, and the non-recyclable materials are directed into the sun to ensure the waste is completely disposed of.
